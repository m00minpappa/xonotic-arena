// Balance — playtested v0.2

Tuned across a 40-game simulation pass. Changes from v0.1: Vortex full damage only at Mid (25 Close/PB · 30 Long) — no more safe-sniping; idfarms gets +4 from 75+ stack with the Action penalty below 50; Bingus spawns with +25 armor; Shrimp hitscan +3 (and −3 while ≥100 stack); flag carriers take −25% damage; CTF is a 10-round match (~20–30 min).

01Setup

Two to six players, free-for-all or in teams. Each player brings one Avatar and one 40-card deck.

  1. Each player picks an Avatar card and a 40-card deck (see §10). In a rated match, players lock Avatars before decks are revealed so the pick can answer the table.
  2. Lay out the map: the 5-location Arena Row for a duel, or the Ring (6 nodes for 3–4 players, 8 for 5–6) for multiplayer — see §08. Seed items onto the map per the Mode Card (§12).
  3. Each Fighter starts with: 100 HP · 0 Armor, Momentum 0, the Blaster equipped for free, and starting ammo Shells 8 · Bullets 20 · Rockets 8 · Cells 12.
  4. Shuffle your deck, draw a hand of 5. Place your Avatar at your spawn location.
  5. Determine first turn: lowest total starting stack, else a coin flip. Thereafter turn order is set by Momentum each round (§08).

02Anatomy of a Turn

A turn has four phases in strict order. You have 2 Actions per turn (a fire or an equip costs 1). Airborne and the Speed powerup each grant +1 Action.

  1. Upkeep. Resolve, in this order:
    • Detonate board tokens you own that trigger now — armed Mortars, burn ticks, Fireball napalm. You may choose to trigger a held Devastator rocket or leave it in flight.
    • Apply Stack decay / regen (§06) and tick any powerup timers you carry.
    • Once per round (on the first player's upkeep), tick every item respawn dial down by 1.
  2. Move phase. Take up to one Move (some tech grants extra). Moving 1 location = +1 Momentum. You may equip weapons from hand here (1 Action each) and grab an item you end on — only if grounded (Momentum ≤ 3).
  3. Fire phase. Spend Actions to fire equipped weapons (ammo cost applies) and play tactic cards. Combos and detonations resolve in the order you choose.
  4. End phase. If you did not Move this turn, −2 Momentum. Then draw back up to 5 cards. If your deck is empty, shuffle your discard into a new deck. Pass to the next player.

// Reactions are off-turn

Reaction cards (Dodge, Air Control, Airshot, Camp Break) are held and played during an opponent's turn in response to their trigger. They do not cost your Actions. One Reaction per trigger unless the card says otherwise.

03Movement & Momentum

Momentum is the whole game. It runs 0–6, and standing still bleeds it away.

  1. Each location moved in your Move phase grants +1 Momentum. Jump-tech cards (Strafe Jump, Bunnyhop, Rocket Jump) add more and may extend how far you move.
  2. At Momentum ≥ 4 you are Airborne: +1 Action, all incoming damage −2 (after armor), and you ignore ground traps (Mines). But you cannot grab items or objectives, and hitscan can still hit you unless you Dodge.
  3. At Momentum ≤ 3 you are Grounded: full damage, but you may grab items and interact with objectives.
  4. Decay: if you don't Move on your turn, lose 2 Momentum in the End phase. Bunnyhop cancels this decay. You cannot remain Airborne without moving.
  5. Knockback shoves a target a number of range-bands directly away from the attacker. Being shoved does not grant Momentum. Shield halves knockback; Strength adds +1 band to yours.

04Combat & Damage

Resolve one hit at a time. Modifiers first, then armor, then the airborne reduction.

  1. Start with the weapon's base damage at the current range band (0 steps = Point-Blank, 1 = Close, 2 = Mid, 3+ = Long). Out-of-range = no effect.
  2. Apply damage modifiers in order: attacker's Strength (×2) and Avatar bonuses, then defender's Shield (×⅓) and Speed (×1.25 taken), then defender Avatar effects. Round down after each step.
  3. Armor split: armor absorbs 60% of the remaining damage — absorbed = min(Armor, ⌊0.6 × D⌋). Reduce Armor by that; the rest hits Health. If Armor can't cover its share, it takes what it can and Health eats the overflow.
  4. If the defender is Airborne, subtract a final 2 from the Health loss (minimum 0).
  5. A Fighter reduced to 0 stack is fragged. In multiplayer the killing blow scores the frag (§08). Respawn follows the Mode Card.

05Weapons, Ammo & Combos

Your weapons are equipment: pick one up once, then use it all game — ammo is the only limit.

  1. Equipping: playing a weapon from hand costs 1 Action and moves it to your Arsenal, face-up, where it stays for the game. The Blaster is equipped free at start.
  2. Firing: costs 1 Action + the weapon's printed ammo. You may fire the same weapon twice in a turn if you have the Actions and ammo. Duplicate weapon copies in hand are spare draws — a second copy is redundant once equipped, so treat it as a discard-for-tempo.
  3. Ammo pools (per type) start at Shells 8 · Bullets 20 · Rockets 8 · Cells 12 and cap at double the start. An Ammo Pack or map pickup refills one type by its starting amount, up to cap.
  4. Board tokens — Blobs, armed Mortars, Mines, in-flight Devastator rockets, Napalm — sit on the map until they trigger. A detonation source (Electro primary, Mortar) pops all Blobs at that location at once.
  5. Reactions interrupt an opponent's fire (see §02). Airshot only triggers against an Airborne target; Camp Break only against a Fighter who skipped their Move.

// Ammo cost per fire

Blaster 0 · Shotgun 1 shell · Machine Gun 2 bullets · Rifle 1 bullet · Mortar / Hagar / Mine Layer 1 rocket · Devastator / Fireball / Seeker 2 rockets · Electro / Crylink 1 cell · Vortex / Arc 2 cells · Vaporizer 3 cells · Tuba 0.

06The Stack & Respawn

Health + Armor, capped at 200 combined. The overstack rots; a low stack heals. This tension is the resource game.

  1. Health starts 100, Armor 0. Health pickups raise Health, Armor pickups raise Armor; neither type alone exceeds 200, and combined stack caps at 200.
  2. Decay (Upkeep): any stack above 100 loses points — 1 per full 25 over 100 (so 150 → −2, 200 → −4). Health rots before Armor.
  3. Regen (Upkeep): if Health is below 100 and you took no damage last turn, regain 10 (below 50, regain 15). Armor never regenerates — only pickups restore it.
  4. Respawn (default): a fragged Fighter returns on their next turn at an open spawn with base stack 100 / 0, starting ammo, and Spawn Shield (§08). You keep your equipped Arsenal — but lose all board tokens and powerups. Modes may override (Clan Arena, Instagib).

// Why you don't just camp the Mega

A 200 stack bleeds 4 per turn and can't be topped up until it drops — so a Mega is fuel for a push now, not a wall to hide behind. Grab it on the way into a fight, not to sit on.

  2. A Fighter may hold one powerup at a time; grabbing a second is not allowed until the first expires. Re-touching the same powerup refreshes its timer.

08Multiplayer & FFA

The two-player Row is a Ring unrolled. Close it into a loop and every proximity rule still holds.

  1. The Ring: locations form a loop (6 nodes for 3–4, 8 for 5–6). Range = the shortest number of steps around the loop between two Fighters. Spawns sit opposite; items on flanks and center.
  2. Initiative: each round, players act in order of highest Momentum first; ties broken by lowest stack (the underdog moves first), then by previous order. Recompute at the start of every round.
  3. Splash & targeting: single-target weapons hit one Fighter in range; splash weapons hit every Fighter at the target location. Knockback can shove a foe into another player's range.
  4. Frag credit: the killing blow scores. Kill-stealing is legal and intended. No assists.
  5. Spawn Shield: a just-respawned Fighter has ½ incoming damage for one turn and can be the target of at most one attacker until they act. Kills spawn-camping.
  6. Bounty (optional FFA): the current frag leader is worth +1 frag to whoever downs them, and their item pickups are halved — a soft check on a runaway lead.
  7. Teams: group by color, friendly fire off (ally splash still knocks, no damage). The Mode win condition is shared; initiative still interleaves both teams by Momentum.

10Deckbuilding & Costing

Constructed play: build a 40-card deck around an Avatar and an ammo identity.

  1. A deck is exactly 40 cards, max 3 copies of any card, max 2 copies of any Powerup (Strength/Shield/Speed/Invisibility), and at most 6 Powerup cards total. Megas, Shards and Ammo Packs are ordinary cards (≤3 each). The Blaster is auto-equipped and not part of the 40.
  2. Weapons drive your ammo economy — a deck should commit to 1–2 ammo types. Splitting four ways starves everything.
  3. Suggested curve: ~10 weapons · ~12 movement/tech · ~8 reactions · ~6 powerups/items · ~4 flex. Adjust to your Avatar (Bingus runs almost no movement; mouseeyeeater runs zero reactions).

The costing framework — five axes

Every weapon is balanced across five knobs; push one up, another must come down. Baseline ideal-range threat ≈ (ammo cost × 12) + 8, then ±20% per axis.
